About ASPCA

The American Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals (ASPCA) is a non-profit organization dedicated to preventing animal cruelty. Founded in 1866, the ASPCA was the first animal welfare organization established in North America.

Mission Statement:
To provide effective means for the prevention of cruelty to animals throughout the United States.

Services:
- Animal rescue and placement
- Animal health services
- Anti-cruelty investigations
- Government relations and advocacy
- Animal behavior and training

Website: https://www.aspca.org

The ASPCA operates the nation's poison control center for animals and provides 24/7 emergency assistance. They also run adoption centers and provide educational resources for pet owners.

Key Programs:
1. ASPCA Animal Poison Control Center
2. ASPCA Behavioral Rehabilitation Center
3. ASPCA Spay/Neuter Alliance
4. ASPCA Field Investigation and Response team

The organization is committed to advancing the safety and well-being of animals through advocacy, education, and hands-on care.
